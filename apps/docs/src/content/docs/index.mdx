## Who this is for

Scientists and contributors who work with stochastic epidemiological models. You can use it to check
your own model before or after you publish, or to follow another paper's model. You do not need to
know programming or AI. Everything here is about the model and the evidence you can check against the
paper.

## What it does

- It starts from a figure in the paper and finds the model behind it: the equations, variables,
  parameters, and starting values.
- For every value, it shows the exact quote and page from the paper — and highlights the spot on the
  page — so you can confirm it against the source. If the paper does not state a value, it says
  "not stated" instead of filling it in.
- _Planned:_ re-drawing the figure from the extracted model to check it reproduces the same result.
  The re-simulation tool is built and tested, but is not yet part of the live extraction run.
- Every step is observable and recorded.
