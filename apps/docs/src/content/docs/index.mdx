## Who this is for

Scientists and contributors who care about high standards in our research community. Whether you're
**checking your own model before (or after) you publish**, or following a colleague's work, this
gives everyone the **same transparent, automated way** to confirm a model reproduces — and to see
exactly where every value came from.

It assumes the work is sound and helps you **show** it. You don't need to know anything about
programming or AI — everything here is about the model and about evidence you can check against the
original paper.

## What it does, in plain terms

- It starts from a **figure** in the paper (a result) and works backward to the **model** behind it:
  the equations, the variables, the parameters, and their starting values.
- For **every value**, it shows the **exact quote and page** from the paper — so anyone can confirm
  it against the source. If the paper never states something, it says so plainly ("not stated")
  rather than filling it in.
- It then **re-draws the figure from the extracted model**. If the re-drawn figure matches, that's
  shared evidence the model was captured faithfully.
- **Every step is observable and recorded**, so the path from paper to model is transparent — the
  same for your work and everyone else's.
