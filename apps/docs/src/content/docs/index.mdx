## Who this is for

You are a researcher evaluating a published model — does it hold up, and **could you reproduce its
results?** This tool reads the stochastic (SDE) epidemiological model *out of* the paper and lays
out, at every step, the evidence you would need to check it yourself.

**You don't need to know anything about programming or AI.** Everything here is about the model and
about proof you can verify against the original paper.

## What it does, in plain terms

- It starts from a **figure** in the paper (a result) and works backward to the **model** behind it:
  the equations, the variables, the parameters, and their starting values.
- For **every value**, it shows the **exact quote and page** from the paper — so you can open the PDF
  and confirm it with your own eyes. If the paper never states something, it says so plainly
  ("not stated") instead of guessing.
- It then **re-draws the figure from the extracted model**. If the re-drawn figure matches the
  paper's, that is direct evidence the model was captured correctly.
- **Every step is observable** — you can watch the data move and see the proof at each point, so the
  whole path from paper to model is something you could follow and repeat.
